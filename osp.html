Overnight Stay Program

The Overnight Stay Program

Now in its 23rd year, OSP is the largest overnight experience program at UC Berkeley attracting nearly 800 participants each year. As an entirely student-run program, we offer participants a unique student-to-student perspective on college life at Cal.

Admitted Cal students (high school seniors who have been accepted to UC Berkeley) can get a taste for college life at UC Berkeley through the Overnight Stay Program—a 24-hour campus visit organized by the students of the Cal Alumni Student Association (CASA).

Parents of admitted students can participate in a concurrent program tailored to parents’ interests, which includes panels of current Cal parents and professors as well as resource presentations regarding financial aid, health and safety, and other campus information.

Program Includes

For students, the cost includes all meals, lodging, and program fees.
For parents, the cost includes all meals and program fees. Accomodations are NOT provided.

During the Overnight Stay Program, you:

  • Explore the UC Berkeley campus and surrounding neighborhoods
  • Eat at residence dining halls
  • Attend class(es) of all subjects and major orientations common to a typical freshman
  • Speak with Cal alumni, professors, and students
  • Stay overnight with a Cal student host in campus dormitories
